================================================================================
TECHNICAL NOTES: PIPELINE EXPANSION & ASTROPHYSICAL SCALING
================================================================================
Topic: Scaling the autonomous modeling framework to the full Kreiner (2004) database.
Date: May 2026

The current pipeline is a high-precision instrument optimized for short-period
(P < 1d) eclipsing binaries. To achieve "Universal Application" across the 
2,000+ systems in the Kreiner database, four critical areas of development 
must be addressed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. MORPHOLOGICAL ADAPTABILITY (EW vs. EB Regimes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CURRENT STATE:
The pipeline uses a "double-ended" logic. For 'EW' (contact) systems, it solves 
for the Fillout Factor (f). For 'EB' (detached) systems, it solves for 
independent fractional radii (R1, R2).

EXPANSION REQUIREMENT:
The most difficult systems in the database are 'Semi-Detached' (SD). In these, 
one star fills its Roche Lobe while the other does not. 
- Fix: The pipeline should implement a "Roche Lobe Audit" at the end of Script 04. 
  If a star's R_equiv is within 1% of its R_Lobe, the script should 
  automatically re-initialize as a Semi-Detached morphology to avoid the 
  "Mesh Overflow" errors seen in early runs.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. TIDAL CIRCULARIZATION & ECCENTRICITY (e)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CURRENT STATE:
Assumption of e = 0.0 (Circular orbit).

ASTROPHYSICAL CONTEXT:
Short-period binaries (P < 1d) are subject to intense tidal forces that 
circularize their orbits over millions of years. This justifies the e=0 
assumption for the current test sample.

EXPANSION REQUIREMENT:
For systems with P > 2.0 days, eccentricity becomes a significant factor. 
- Impact: A non-zero 'e' changes the shape and timing of the secondary eclipse. 
- Fix: Add 'ecc' (eccentricity) and 'omega' (argument of periastron) to the 
  MCMC free parameters. This increases the dimensionality of the problem 
  from 5D to 7D, requiring ~4,000 iterations for convergence.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. TEMPERATURE ANCHORING & GAIA DR3 INTEGRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CURRENT STATE:
Primary temperature (T1) is anchored via a static lookup dictionary 
(SPECTRAL_TYPE_TEFF) based on the GCVS/VSX classification.

LIMITATION:
Catalog spectral types (e.g., "A5") are often based on ground-based surveys from 
decades ago and may have uncertainties of ±500K.

EXPANSION REQUIREMENT:
Integrated Gaia DR3 API calls.
- Fix: Use the 'astroquery.gaia' module to pull the G-band magnitude and 
  BP-RP color index. This allows the pipeline to derive a "Broadband 
  Photometric Temperature" for T1 that is significantly more accurate than 
  simple catalog lookups.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THIRD-LIGHT CONTAMINATION (l3) & TESS PIXEL SCALE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CURRENT STATE:
Assumption of l3 = 0.0 (Zero contamination).

LIMITATION:
TESS has a massive pixel scale (~21 arcsec/pixel). In crowded star fields 
(especially near the Galactic Plane), light from background stars frequently 
leaks into the binary's aperture.

ASTROPHYSICAL IMPACT:
Third-light "dilutes" the light curve, making eclipses appear shallower. 
This forces the MCMC to find a solution with a smaller radius or lower 
inclination than is physically true (Systematic Bias).

EXPANSION REQUIREMENT:
- Fix: Implement 'l3_mode=fraction' in Script 05. The optimizer should 
  attempt to solve for 'l3' as a free parameter, bounded by the TESS 
  CROWDSAP metric provided in the SPOC header files.

================================================================================
SUMMARY FOR THESIS DOCUMENTATION
================----------------------------------------------------------------
These considerations demonstrate the transition from a "Specific Case" study to 
a "Generalized Framework." Documenting these points proves the scalability of 
the computational methodology and acknowledges the inherent physical 
complexity of the stellar population within the Kreiner database.
